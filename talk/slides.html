Designing a Student-Centered Open Textbook for Differential Equations

Blending Narrative, Technology, and Pedagogy

Dr. Geoff Cox, Dr. Blain Patterson
Applied Mathematics, Virginia Military Institute

Last Updated: December 28, 2025

Abstract

Ordinary differential equations (ODEs) form a gateway course for many STEM majors, yet students often encounter steep conceptual and technical barriers when transitioning from calculus to differential equations. This talk presents a case study in rethinking ODE pedagogy through the development of an open-access textbook that blends student-centered narrative, scaffolded checkpoints, interactive technology, and structured workflows.

The project‚Äôs guiding question is: How can textbook design itself function as an innovation in teaching and learning? Each chapter is built around conversational explanations, step-by-step workflow and reading checkpoints that emphasize concepts and procedure. Interactive visualizations add a dynamic perspective to simulate a classroom experience.

By framing the textbook not only as a resource but as a pedagogical experiment, this work highlights the role of curriculum design in shaping student experiences of ODEs and opens space for dialogue about how innovative resources can transform undergraduate mathematics education.

Part I: PreTeXt - What is PreTeXt?

PreTeXt is an open-source authoring language designed for writing textbooks, articles, lecture notes, and course materials.

‚ú¶ Write once, publish everywhere

From one source you can produce web, PDF, ePub, and Braille versions.

‚ú¶ Built-in Interactive Components:

Suppliment narrative with interactive reading questions, coding tasks, or visualization tools.

‚ú¶ Accessibility Out of the Box:

PreText is Screen-Reader Friendly and supports Braille, Large Print, and Dark/Light Mode.

Whether you want a polished textbook, an online & interactive research hub, or just clean online lecture notes, PreTeXt is ready for just about any workflow.
